The verb "To Have"

In English, the verb "to have" is used to indicate possession or ownership. It can also be used to talk about relationships, experiences, and physical sensations. Here is the basic structure of "to have" in the present tense:

  • I have
  • You have
  • He/She/It has
  • We have
  • They have

Examples:

1. Possession: I have a car.
2. Relationships: She has a brother.
3. Experiences: They have traveled to Japan.
4. Physical sensations: He has a headache.
